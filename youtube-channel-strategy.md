# YouTube Channel Strategy — Deep Analysis of Vaibhav Sisinty + Your Own Channel Blueprint

> Research date: June 2026. Channel analyzed: [@vaibhavsisinty](https://youtube.com/@vaibhavsisinty)
> Goal: reverse-engineer how he creates content, then build a complete, executable plan for launching your own channel.

---

## PART 1 — How Vaibhav Sisinty's Content Machine Actually Works

### 1.1 Who he is (the context that explains everything)

| Fact | Detail |
|---|---|
| Role | Founder/CEO of GrowthSchool + OutSkill (AI upskilling ed-tech) |
| Scale | 10M+ learners across 45+ countries, 150+ employees |
| YouTube | ~230K–300K+ subscribers (late 2025 → 2026) |
| Instagram | ~2M followers (claims 800K gained in 8 months) |
| LinkedIn | 130K+ followers, 25M+ post views — this is where he started |
| Background | Growth lead at Uber and Klook before founding GrowthSchool |

**The single most important insight:** his content is NOT the business. Content is the
**top of a sales funnel**. Every video, reel, and post exists to drive people toward a
free masterclass/5-day workshop, which converts into paid cohort courses. He is a
growth hacker first, creator second. Understanding this changes how you read every
choice he makes.

### 1.2 The funnel (his real "content strategy")

```
Short-form content (Reels/Shorts) — awareness, millions of views
        ↓
Long-form YouTube (weekly AI updates, masterclasses) — trust & authority
        ↓
Podcast guesting (Raj Shamani FO, Josh Talks, etc.) — borrowed audiences + credibility
        ↓
Free lead magnet: "Free AI Masterclass" / "5-Day LinkedIn Workshop"
        ↓
Email + WhatsApp community nurture
        ↓
Paid cohort programs (GrowthSchool / OutSkill)
```

Every piece of content has a job in this ladder. Nothing is random.

### 1.3 Content pillars (what he talks about)

1. **AI tools & workflows** — "my exact daily AI tool stack", tool roundups, prompts.
2. **Make money / save time with AI** — "Start Earning Money With AI", business ideas.
3. **Career fear + opportunity** — "AI will replace X, here's how to survive" framing.
4. **Founder/growth lessons** — scaling stories, growth hacking tactics.
5. **AI news, decoded** — weekly "what happened in AI and why you should care."

Note the emotional engine: **FOMO + practicality**. Every topic is either "you're
falling behind" (fear) or "steal this and win" (greed/aspiration). That duo drives
clicks in the self-improvement/AI niche.

### 1.4 Formats and how each platform is used

| Format | Platform | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 30–60s talking-head reels, fast cuts, bold captions | Instagram/Shorts | Reach. Volume game (near-daily) |
| Weekly long-form AI update videos | YouTube | Authority + subscriber base |
| Masterclass-style long videos (40min+) | YouTube | Pre-selling the paid course experience |
| Podcast guest appearances | Other channels | Borrowing big audiences (Raj Shamani's FO episodes have millions of views) |
| Text posts / carousels | LinkedIn | B2B credibility, lead gen |

### 1.5 The AI-first production system (the part most people miss)

This is his openly stated edge — he says **"99% of my content is AI-made"**:

- **Research:** Perplexity (quick), Gemini Deep Research (deep dives)
- **Scriptwriting:** ChatGPT for writing; a **Claude Project trained on his past video scripts** writes his reel scripts *in his voice*
- **Brainstorming:** OpenAI o3-class reasoning models
- **Long-form video:** he uses an **AI clone (digital twin/avatar)** to ship weekly
  long-form AI update videos on YouTube without filming every week
- **Post-production:** AI for cleanup of delivery, auto-captioning, repurposing long
  clips into shorts

Result: founder-level output (running a 150-person company) while shipping
near-daily content with a tiny team. **He productized himself.** One recording
session or one script feeds 5+ pieces of content across 4 platforms.

### 1.6 Hook & packaging patterns (steal these mechanics, not the words)

- **Imperative + theft framing:** "Steal this AI prompt…"
- **Money + specificity:** "Start Earning Money With AI — Free Tools & Business Ideas"
- **Numbers everywhere:** "5 tools", "10x your speed", "800K followers in 8 months"
- **Stakes in the first 3 seconds:** the cost of NOT knowing this
- **Free value with a cliff:** the content genuinely teaches, but the *complete*
  system always lives one step deeper (the free masterclass → the paid cohort)
- **CTA style:** "comment [WORD] and I'll DM you the doc" — comment-to-DM automation
  that converts viewers into email/WhatsApp leads

### 1.7 What's genuinely replicable vs. what isn't

**Replicable:** the funnel thinking, AI production stack, hook patterns, repurposing
pipeline, posting cadence, comment-to-DM lead capture.

**Not replicable (yet):** his distribution head start (LinkedIn audience built since
~2019), the credibility of "founder of a 10M-learner company", podcast invitations,
and a team. Your plan must compensate with **niche focus and consistency**, not scale.

## PART 2 — Your Own Channel: The Whole Process & Solid Plan

### 2.1 Step 0 — Positioning (decide this before recording anything)

Vaibhav wins because "AI for professionals" matches his business. Copying his niche
puts you against him and 10,000 others. Use the **intersection formula**:

> (a skill you genuinely have) × (AI angle) × (a specific audience)

Candidate positions — pick ONE:

1. **AI × Trading/Investing** *(strong fit given your existing work in options,
   F&O, BankNifty, valuation)* — "I build AI tools and systems for Indian traders."
   Underserved, high-intent audience, natural product path (indicators, courses,
   communities).
2. **AI × your day-job domain** — "AI workflows for [engineers / marketers / etc.]"
3. **Build-in-public** — documenting building your own AI/trading product weekly.

Write a one-line promise and put it in the channel banner, e.g.:
*"I test AI tools and trading systems with real money so you don't have to."*

**Differentiation rule:** Vaibhav sells breadth ("AI for everyone"). You win with
depth ("AI for THIS person, with receipts"). Show real numbers, real backtests, real
screen recordings — proof is the moat AI content cannot fake.

### 2.2 Channel foundations (Week 0 — one weekend)

- [ ] Handle + name: clear > clever. Your name or "[Name] — [Niche]".
- [ ] Banner: the one-line promise + upload schedule.
- [ ] About section: who it's for, what they get, cadence, one CTA.
- [ ] 30–45s channel trailer: who you are, the promise, what to watch first.
- [ ] Set up the lead capture from day 1: a free PDF/Notion doc (lead magnet)
      + a simple email list (Beehiiv/ConvertKit free tier). Even at 0 subs —
      the funnel mindset is the lesson from Part 1.

### 2.3 Content pillars & weekly cadence

Use a 3-pillar system mapped to the funnel:

| Pillar | Job | Format | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Discovery** — trending tools/news in your niche, decoded | Reach new people | Shorts/Reels 30–60s | 4–5/week |
| **Authority** — deep tutorials, systems, case studies with proof | Convert viewers → subscribers | Long-form 8–15 min | 1/week |
| **Trust** — results, failures, behind-the-scenes, "what I'd do if starting today" | Convert subscribers → fans/leads | Long-form or short | 2/month (counts toward the above) |

Sustainable target: **1 long-form + 4 shorts per week.** Vaibhav-style leverage rule:
**never create a short from scratch** — every short is cut from the weekly long-form
or from one weekly 30-minute "talking session" you batch-record.

### 2.4 The AI production system (your version of his machine)

Weekly pipeline, ~6–8 hours total:

1. **Research (45 min):** Perplexity + Gemini/ChatGPT deep research on the week's
   topic; collect 3 data points or a personal test result as proof.
2. **Script (60 min):** Create a **Claude Project** seeded with your 5 best scripts
   and a style guide ("simple words, short sentences, hook in line 1, one idea per
   video"). It drafts; you rewrite the hook and add your proof. *This mirrors his
   exact setup.*
3. **Record (90 min, batched):** one sitting — the long-form + hook lines for shorts.
   Phone camera + window light + a ₹1,500 lapel mic is enough. For screen-heavy
   trading/AI content, OBS screen recording + face bubble works from day 1.
4. **Edit (2–3 hrs):** Descript (edit video by editing text, remove filler words) or
   CapCut. Auto-captions ON for all shorts. Later: outsource or use OpusClip/Klap to
   auto-cut shorts from the long-form.
5. **Package (45 min):** 5 title options (pick by curiosity + specificity), thumbnail
   in Canva — 3 words max, face + emotion, high contrast.
6. **Distribute (30 min):** YouTube long-form → 3–4 Shorts → same verticals to
   Instagram Reels → key insight as a LinkedIn text post. One effort, four surfaces.
7. **(Later, optional)** AI avatar/clone via HeyGen for news-update videos once your
   audience accepts your face/voice — exactly the sequencing Vaibhav used: human
   first, clone after trust exists.

### 2.5 Hook/title/thumbnail playbook

Formulas (adapted from his patterns to a proof-driven channel):

- "I tested [tool/strategy] for 30 days. Here's the data."
- "Steal my exact [workflow/system] for [outcome]"
- "[Big claim] — and I can prove it" / "Nobody is talking about [X]"
- "5 [tools/setups] that actually [outcome] in 2026 (tested)"
- First 15 seconds of every long-form: **stake → proof → roadmap**
  ("Here's what this is worth to you, here's why you can trust me on it, here's
  what's coming.") No intros, no "hey guys welcome back."

### 2.6 The 90-day launch plan

**Phase 1 — Foundation (Days 1–14)**
- Lock niche, set up channel, build the Claude Project + script templates.
- Record the trailer + first long-form + first 4 shorts. Publish nothing yet;
  build a 2-week buffer.

**Phase 2 — Volume & calibration (Days 15–60)**
- Ship the cadence: 1 long + 4 shorts/week, zero exceptions.
- Weekly 30-min review: CTR (target >4%), average view duration (>40% on long-form),
  Shorts swipe-away rate. Double down on the top pillar, kill the worst.
- Start comment-to-DM/lead-magnet CTAs around Day 30.

**Phase 3 — Leverage (Days 61–90)**
- Take your 2 best-performing videos and make 3 variations of each (new angle,
  deeper version, "part 2").
- Begin small collabs: comment meaningfully on bigger niche channels, pitch 2–3
  podcast/livestream guest spots (his "borrowed audience" play, at your scale).
- Introduce the monetization ladder's first rung (below).

**Expectations check:** 90 days of this typically yields 1K–10K subs in a good
niche — not 800K in 8 months. He had a decade of audience-building and a company
behind that stat. Judge yourself on shipping rate and retention curves, not subs.

### 2.7 Monetization ladder (in order — don't skip rungs)

1. **Days 1–90:** Nothing for sale. Lead magnet + email list only.
2. **~1K subs:** Affiliates for tools you genuinely use (AI tools, brokers, data
   platforms pay well).
3. **~5K subs:** A paid digital product (₹500–2,000 / $10–50): prompt pack, system
   template, backtest library — productize your best free video.
4. **~10K+ subs:** Community or cohort (the Vaibhav model): free workshop → paid
   program. Also: YouTube Partner Program revenue (a bonus, never the plan).

### 2.8 Tool stack & budget

| Job | Tool | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Research | Perplexity free + ChatGPT/Gemini | ₹0–2,000/mo |
| Scripts | Claude (Project trained on your scripts) | ~₹1,700/mo |
| Recording | Phone + lapel mic + OBS (screen) | ~₹1,500 one-time |
| Editing | CapCut / Descript | ₹0–1,300/mo |
| Shorts auto-cutting | OpusClip / Klap (from month 2–3) | ~₹2,500/mo |
| Thumbnails | Canva | ₹0–500/mo |
| Email list | Beehiiv / ConvertKit free tier | ₹0 |
| AI avatar (much later) | HeyGen | ~₹2,500/mo |

Start under **₹5,000/month**. Upgrade only what the analytics justify.

### 2.9 First 10 video ideas (AI × Trading positioning example)

1. I made ChatGPT analyze BankNifty for 30 days — here's what happened (long)
2. Steal my exact AI prompt for analyzing any stock in 2 minutes (short)
3. The 5 AI tools every Indian trader needs in 2026 — tested (long)
4. AI built my entire options strategy. I backtested it. (long)
5. Why 90% of AI trading advice on Instagram is fake — with proof (short)
6. My complete AI research workflow before every trade (long)
7. ChatGPT vs Claude vs Gemini: which reads charts best? (long)
8. This free tool backtests any strategy in 5 minutes (short)
9. I asked AI to find me an undervalued stock — then ran the valuation myself (long)
10. The exact system I use to turn 1 video into 7 pieces of content (short — meta, builds creator credibility)

### 2.10 The 5 rules that matter more than everything above

1. **Funnel before fame** — every video should move someone one step (viewer →
   subscriber → email → customer). Vaibhav's entire empire is this one idea.
2. **Volume with leverage** — batch once, publish everywhere. AI writes drafts;
   you supply the hook, the proof, and the personality.
3. **Proof is the differentiator** — real numbers, real tests, real losses. It's the
   one thing an AI-flooded niche can't commoditize.
4. **Package > production** — a great title/thumbnail on an okay video beats the
   reverse. Spend disproportionate time on the first 3 seconds and the thumbnail.
5. **Survive 90 days** — the algorithm rewards consistency compounding. Most
   channels die at video 8. The plan above is designed so week 12 costs the same
   energy as week 1.
