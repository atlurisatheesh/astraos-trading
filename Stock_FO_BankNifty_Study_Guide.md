# Stock, F&O, Bank Nifty, and Options Study Guide

Last updated: March 25, 2026

## Purpose
This guide combines the strongest books, official India-specific learning resources, and a practical roadmap for building serious skill in:
- stock analysis
- long-term investing
- F&O
- Bank Nifty and Nifty trading
- options
- intraday trading
- derivatives risk and execution

Important note:
- There is no universally respected, elite, publisher-backed "best Bank Nifty book" comparable to the best global books on valuation or options.
- For Bank Nifty and Indian F&O, the strongest path is to combine world-class global books with official Indian market resources from NSE, NISM, and Zerodha Varsity.

## Part 1: Top 10 Advanced Books for Stocks and Equity Investing

### 1. Security Analysis, 7th Edition
- Authors: Benjamin Graham, David Dodd, Seth Klarman
- Best for: deep fundamental equity analysis and classic value-investing discipline
- Why read it: still one of the strongest serious books for thinking about stocks rigorously
- Link: https://www.mheducation.com/highered/mhp/product/security-analysis-seventh-edition.html

### 2. Investment Valuation, 4th Edition
- Author: Aswath Damodaran
- Best for: professional-grade valuation
- Why read it: excellent for discounted cash flow, multiples, difficult cases, and valuation judgment
- Link: https://play.google.com/store/books/details/Aswath_Damodaran_Investment_Valuation?id=CdA5EQAAQBAJ

### 3. Financial Statement Analysis for Value Investing
- Authors: Stephen Penman, Peter F. Pope
- Best for: turning accounting into investment decisions
- Why read it: one of the strongest books for serious financial statement analysis
- Link: https://cup.columbia.edu/book/financial-statement-analysis-for-value-investing/9780231215688/

### 4. Valuation: Measuring and Managing the Value of Companies, 8th Edition
- Authors: Tim Koller, Marc Goedhart, David Wessels, McKinsey & Company
- Best for: institutional-style company valuation
- Why read it: outstanding for business analysis, value drivers, and forecasting
- Link: https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/strategy-and-corporate-finance/our-insights/valuation-measuring-and-managing-the-value-of-companies

### 5. Financial Shenanigans
- Authors: Howard Schilit, Jeremy Perler, Yoni Engelhart
- Best for: spotting accounting manipulation and reporting red flags
- Why read it: protects you from low-quality companies and fake earnings quality
- Link: https://www.mheducation.com/highered/mhp/product/financial-shenanigans-fourth-edition.html

### 6. Common Stocks and Uncommon Profits and Other Writings
- Author: Philip A. Fisher
- Best for: business quality, scuttlebutt, and long-term stock selection
- Why read it: teaches how to identify exceptional companies, not just statistically cheap ones
- Link: https://www.wiley-vch.de/en/areas-interest/finance-economics-law/common-stocks-and-uncommon-profits-and-other-writings-978-0-471-44550-0

### 7. Expected Returns
- Author: Antti Ilmanen
- Best for: understanding return drivers, factors, and market behavior
- Why read it: powerful for thinking like a professional allocator
- Link: https://qa.store.wiley.com/en-us/Expected%2BReturns%3A%2BAn%2BInvestor%27s%2BGuide%2Bto%2BHarvesting%2BMarket%2BRewards-p-x000568799

### 8. Asset Management: A Systematic Approach to Factor Investing
- Author: Andrew Ang
- Best for: factors, portfolio construction, and systematic investing
- Why read it: helps connect stock picking to portfolio-level decision-making
- Link: https://academic.oup.com/book/3342

### 9. Trading and Exchanges: Market Microstructure for Practitioners
- Author: Larry Harris
- Best for: understanding how markets actually function
- Why read it: essential for traders who want to understand execution, liquidity, and order flow
- Link: https://academic.oup.com/book/52292

### 10. The Intelligent Investor, Revised Edition
- Author: Benjamin Graham
- Best for: investment philosophy and temperament
- Why read it: not the most technical, but still one of the most important books for long-term market survival
- Link: https://www.harperacademic.com/book/9780062417619/the-intelligent-investor-rev-ed

## Part 5: 90-Day Study Roadmap for Bank Nifty and Options

## Days 1-30: Build the foundation

### Goals
- understand derivatives basics
- understand futures vs options
- understand Indian market structure
- learn chart basics and intraday structure

### Study
- Zerodha Varsity: Technical Analysis
- Zerodha Varsity: Futures Trading
- NSE NCFM Derivatives Market (Dealers) Module

### Daily work
- mark previous day high and low
- mark opening range
- watch Bank Nifty spot and nearest weekly expiry
- observe ATM, ITM, and OTM pricing behavior
- maintain one notebook or spreadsheet for observations

### Weekly outcome
- Week 1: understand contract basics and how Bank Nifty moves
- Week 2: understand support, resistance, trend, and range
- Week 3: understand futures, leverage, and margin logic
- Week 4: classify daily sessions into trend, range, gap, and reversal

## Days 31-60: Learn options properly

### Goals
- understand option pricing
- learn Greeks and implied volatility
- understand open interest and option-chain behavior
- understand time decay near expiry

### Study
- Zerodha Varsity: Options Theory
- NSE NCFM Options Trading Strategies Module
- Option Volatility and Pricing

### Daily work
- compare ATM, ITM, and OTM reactions
- write down one bullish and one bearish options idea every day
- track delta, theta, and IV behavior into expiry
- paper-trade only, no random live experimentation

### Weekly outcome
- Week 5: understand payoff and moneyness
- Week 6: understand Greeks deeply
- Week 7: understand IV and OI interaction
- Week 8: understand simple spreads and risk-defined structures

## Days 61-90: Build a repeatable Bank Nifty process

### Goals
- identify the day type before entering
- build 2-3 repeatable setups
- define risk rules
- learn to avoid low-quality trades

### Study
- NSE NCFM Options Trading (Advanced) Module
- Trading Options Greeks
- Mind Over Markets
- Trading in the Zone

### Daily work
- identify whether the day is trend, range, gap continuation, or failed breakout
- create entry, stop-loss, target, and invalidation before every paper trade
- review trades based on process quality, not only P&L
- refine only a small number of setups

### Weekly outcome
- Week 9: build a trend-day playbook
- Week 10: build a range-day playbook
- Week 11: build an expiry-day caution framework
- Week 12: create a written trading rulebook

## Part 7: Best 3 Setups to Master First for Bank Nifty

Do not try to learn everything at once. Master these first:

### 1. Trend-day setup
- use opening structure, VWAP, higher high/higher low logic, and option-chain support
- aim to participate in continuation, not guess tops and bottoms

### 2. Opening range breakout or breakout-failure setup
- useful when Bank Nifty establishes early directional intent
- especially powerful when aligned with index futures and sector strength

### 3. Range-day premium strategy or defined-risk spread
- use only when the market is clearly non-trending
- avoid naked aggression if you do not fully understand Greeks and adjustment risk

## Part 8: Daily Practice Checklist

Before market:
- note global cues
- note SGX/GIFT Nifty if relevant to your workflow
- mark major support and resistance
- note expiry proximity
- note scheduled events and major results

At market open:
- observe opening range
- compare spot with futures and key option strikes
- identify whether buyers or sellers are in control

During market:
- watch price relative to VWAP
- watch whether OI supports or rejects the move
- avoid revenge trades
- do not trade if the day type is unclear

After market:
- journal every trade
- classify the setup
- note whether entry was valid
- note whether stop-loss discipline was followed
- review errors separately from good losses

## Part 10: Final Recommendation

If your target is:

### Long-term investing
- focus more on Security Analysis, Investment Valuation, Valuation, Penman, Fisher, and Financial Shenanigans

### Bank Nifty intraday and options
- focus more on Zerodha Varsity, NSE derivatives modules, Natenberg, Passarelli, Mind Over Markets, and Trading in the Zone

### Becoming very strong overall
- combine both tracks, but learn options and intraday execution separately from long-term stock investing

The strongest path is:
- learn structure first
- learn options pricing second
- learn risk third
- only then increase complexity
